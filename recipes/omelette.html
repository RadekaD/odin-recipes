French Omelette

Description

A true French omelette, or omelet as we Americans call it, is just eggs and butter, no filling. The egg is folded for a soft, tender texture. It's 10% ingredients and 90% technique, so it does take a bit of practice to perfect.

Ingredients

  • 3 large fresh eggs
  • ½ teaspoon cold water
  • ¼ teaspoon kosher salt
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, divided
  • 1 pinch cayenne or white pepper to taste (Optional)

Steps

  1. Whisk together oil, soy sauce, vinegar, lemon juice, Worcestershire sauce, Dijon mustard, garlic, and pepper for marinade in a 9x13-inch glass baking dish until thoroughly combined.
  2. Add flank steak to the baking dish; turn several times to coat thoroughly with marinade. Cover, and refrigerate for 2 to 6 hours, or up to 12 hours if you have time.
  3. Make the ricotta mixture.
  4. When ready to cook, preheat an outdoor grill for medium-high heat and lightly oil the grate.
  5. Remove steak from the marinade and shake off excess. Discard the remaining marinade.
  6. Cook steak on the preheated grill for about 5 minutes per side, or to desired doneness.
  7. Remove from the grill and let rest for 5 minutes before slicing and serving.
